Hell

Epilogus

Sometimes referred to as GreatCorn's Hell, the 10-Tier Hell or 87 Hell, it shows itself as somewhat of an reinterpretation of Dante Alighieri's Divine Comedy. The structure combines the 9 circles of hell from Inferno and the 7 deadly sins from Purgatorio. The concept is used in many GreatCorn's creations of many types - from music to digital artwork and videogames. GreatCorn also includes small concepts of his own Purgatorio and Paradiso.

GreatCorn's hell begins similar to Dante's Inferno, except the first circle/tier is Acheron, then comes Limbo. The sixth circle, which according to the Divine Comedy should've been Wrath and Styx, becomes Sloth. Then, the positioning continues through by the 7 Deadly Sins and the 10th tier is Cocytus. Each circle has a theme, its guardian, icon, color and souls, suffering inside. However, that may differ in relation to the art form. In some forms, the guardians are not present (e.g. no need, as in sheet music). When they are, they are sorted by Peter Binsfeld's Classification of Demons. In Acheron and Cocytus the guardian is Egonus, a demon of GreatCorn himself.

The concept first appeared in 87 - An Epilogue, which first was intended to be based completely on the 9 circles concept by Dathe Alighieri.

The purgatory concept is not fully developed. It was first featured in DREATH. It is told in Peccata Mea (Somnus) that Egonus took residence in the 10th tier of hell, on the frozen lake Cocytus.
